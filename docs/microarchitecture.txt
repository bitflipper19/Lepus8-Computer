1. The microarchitecture is seperated into two portions:
       a. Datapath: Comprises of all the bus, registers, buffers, computation 
          units like the ALU, etc.

       b. Control Unit: An FSM based control unit which will take the contents
          IR (Instruction Register) as input and outputs the Control Word or
          the micro-operations necessary for execution

2. The Datapath is further split into three parts: 
       a. Fetch Unit: Calculates the address, puts it into the PC (Program Counter)
          then the MAR (Memory Address Register) takes the address and sends it to the
          memory.
          The Memory then outputs the Instruction into MDR (Memory Data Register)
          which then writes it to the IR.
       b. Register File: REG_A, REG_B, REG_C, SP, BP, DI, FR, OR
       c. ALU 

3. All units (except the Control Unit) put their Data on the Bus 
   and all units take data from the bus.

4. Microoperations:

      AO register A out
      AI register A in 
      ALU_O ALU register out
      ALU_A_IN ALU register A in
      ALU_B_IN ALU register B in

      IR_LB_IN IR Lower Byte in
      IR_HB_IN IR higher Byte in 
      IR_LB_OUT IR lower byte out
